Jamican Beef Stew

my go-to recipe in combination with any kind of rice

I learnt how to prepare this stew form my mom, this stew had always been our sunday special back home in Nigeria. This is a stew rich in protien with the chunky amount of beef being used in its preparation and it also has a firey end tone taste of bell and black pepper. It is generally spicy and delicious, we usually combine it with white or brown rice.

Here are the ingredients for this amazing stew

  • Beef
  • Bell Pepper
  • Soy sauce
  • White onion
  • Thyme
  • Carrots
  • Bay leaves
  • Flour
  • Olive oil
  • Brown sugar
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Rice
  • Allspice
  • Steak sauce
  • Tomatoes

Preparation steps

  1. Season beef with salt and pepper. Add beef to a pan and cook until well-browned on one side, about 5 minutes. Stir to loosen meat, continue to adding onions and carrot, reduce heat to medium and add habaneros, garlic and rum.
  2. Bring to a boil and simmer for 2 minutes before adding chicken broth, allspice, brown sugar, red wine vinegar, tomatoes, soy sauce, and steak sauce.
  3. Continue cooking until meat is tear-apart tender when pireced with a fork, when stew is done, discard thyme springs and bay leaves. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Garnish with green onions and serve hot with white or brown rice.

I love this stew because of its high protien value and the firey hot sensation it gives.
